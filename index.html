About Me

I am a Staff Scientist working on the Advanced Camera for Surveys (ACS) team within the Instruments Division at STScI. ACS is a third-generation Hubble Space Telescope instrument, and is one of the two operational imaging cameras on the spacecraft.

I was a graduate student at the Institute for Astronomy at the University of Hawaii, where I worked with my advisor Brent Tully on nearby galaxies, the distance ladder, and large-scale structure.

I received my A.B. in Astronomy and Physics at Vassar College, where I worked under Professor Fred Chromey on both observational and theoretical studies of contact binary systems. I also spent a summer working under Professor Elizabeth McGrath at Colby College, where I studied the evolution of massive quiescent galaxies using CANDELS. I then attended Boston University, where I obtained my M.A. and studied optical, infrared, and x-ray emission from clusters of galaxies with Professor Elizabeth Blanton.

Recent Highlights

A few highlights from my recent work:

Comparing Tip of the Red Giant Branch Distance Scales (Anand+22)

Here we present an independent reanalysis of the Carnegie Chicago Hubble Program's HST dataset and use a NGC 4258 -> TRGB -> SNe Ia distance ladder to find H0 = 71.5 +/- 1.8 km/s/Mpc.

A Comprehensive Measurement of the Hubble Constant from the SH0ES Team (Riess+22)

We report observations from HST of Cepheid variables in the host galaxies of 42 Type SNe Ia used to calibrate the Hubble constant and find H0 = 73.04 ± 1.04 km/s/Mpc, a a 5σ difference with the prediction of HH0 from Planck cosmic microwave background observations under ΛCDM.

The Extragalactic Distance Database: CMDs/TRGB Distance Catalog (Anand+21b)

Here we provide a decadal update to the Extragalactic Distance Database's Color Magnitude Diagram/Tip of the Red Giant Branch Distance catalog, which has now doubled in size to over 500 galaxies.

Distances to PHANGS Galaxies (Anand+21a)

We present a catalog of distances for the PHANGS program, including new TRGB distances that are derived from PHANGS-HST imaging.
